About

I am a PhD candidate in the Data Science for Digital Health (DS4DH) group at the University of Geneva, working under the supervision of Prof. Douglas Teodoro. My doctoral research focuses on leveraging state-of-the-art natural language processing (NLP) methodologies to extract structured information from multilingual biomedical texts.

My work involves transforming unstructured data from diverse linguistic sources into coherent, structured formats to enhance the utility of medical information in research and healthcare improvement initiatives. I am particularly interested in biomedical concept normalization, word sense disambiguation, and evaluating how well language models represent medical concepts in context.

Prior to my PhD, I worked as a Research Scientist at Dadmatech in Tehran, Iran, where I focused on transforming English texts into structured formats using advanced deep learning techniques, including entity typing and named entity recognition. I completed my Master's degree at Shahid Beheshti University, working with Dr. Mehrnoush Shamsfard on multilingual word sense disambiguation and building the first Persian WSD corpus.
